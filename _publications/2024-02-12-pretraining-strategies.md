Machine-learning models, especially graph neural networks (GNNs), enable fast and accurate prediction of material properties, but most rely on relaxed crystal structures that are expensive and time-consuming to generate. This work develops **pretraining strategies for structure-agnostic property prediction**, allowing models to learn from unrelaxed structures and improving generalization for high-throughput materials screening.
