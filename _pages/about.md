I am a Ph.D. student in **Mechanical Engineering** at the **University of Michigan**, where I work with [Prof. Venkat Viswanathan](https://micde.umich.edu/member/venkat-viswanathan/). Before Michigan, I earned my M.S. in AI Engineering for Materials at **Carnegie Mellon University**, advised by [Prof. Amir Barati Farimani](https://sites.google.com/view/barati/) in the Mechanical and AI Lab.

My research focus is **AI for Science** — building machine-learning and agentic systems that accelerate scientific discovery. This work is **interdisciplinary by design**, sitting at the intersection of materials science, mechanical engineering, computational physics, high-performance computing, and machine learning. My goal is to turn slow, expensive simulation and screening pipelines into fast, autonomous tools for designing new materials.

That perspective grew across each stage of my training. As an undergraduate in Materials Science and Engineering at the **Guangdong Technion–Israel Institute of Technology**, I worked with Prof. Nan Wang to develop a phase-field model for binary-alloy solidification, accelerating it with MPI-based parallel computing. At CMU, I shifted toward data-driven methods, building transformer and language-model approaches for interpretable materials property prediction ([MatInFormer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.16259)). At Michigan, I now focus on agentic AI — multi-agent LLM systems that plan and run first-principles materials simulations end to end ([DREAMS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.14267)).

Research Focus: AI for Science
------
- **Agentic & LLM systems** — autonomous, multi-agent frameworks for materials simulation and discovery
- **Machine learning for materials** — transformers, graph neural networks, and pretraining for property prediction
- **Computational materials science** — phase-field modeling, density functional theory, and high-throughput screening
- **High-performance computing** — parallel computing (MPI) and scalable simulation workflows
